# sample_inputs/

Fictional Acme Corp QBR inputs used to exercise the Meeting Prep Assistant Gem. Everything here is invented — no real company, logo, or brand is referenced.

## Files

- **`notes.pdf`** — 3 pages. Internal account manager scratchpad before a Quarterly Business Review (QBR). Informal voice, bullets, parenthetical asides. Meant to read like real CRM / Notion prep notes, not polished prose.
- **`slides.pptx`** — 8 slides. The customer-facing deck the account team plans to present at the QBR. Polished, slightly salesy tone. Kept as the canonical deliverable.
- **`slides.pdf`** — 8 pages, one per slide. PDF export of `slides.pptx` for upload into Google AI Studio (which accepts PDF more reliably than PPTX). Layout, palette, and page numbers mirror the .pptx.

## Scenario

- **Account:** Acme Corp, mid-market SaaS (~800 employees), customer for 18 months.
- **Our side:** Account Manager at a data platform vendor. Product ingests Acme's operational data and powers their internal analytics.
- **Contract:** $1.2M ARR, renewal due in 90 days, auto-renew clause we do not want invoked.
- **Meeting purpose:** QBR. Our stated goal is smooth renewal. Their stated goal is value review + Q+1 planning. Several signals in the notes suggest they may be price-shopping.
- **Stakeholders:** Sarah Chen (VP Data, champion), Marcus Webb (CTO, quiet supporter), Elena Rodriguez (CFO, now attending unprompted), Dev Patel (Head of Data Science, new, stance unclear).

## Designed contradictions

The two documents deliberately disagree on several points. The Gem's job — especially in the V3 adversarial iteration — is to notice and surface these, not smooth them over. Primary contradictions:

1. **P1 ticket resolution.** Slide 5 says "average ticket resolution: 18 hours". Notes p.2 records that last month's two P1 tickets took **~48 hours** to resolve, breaching the 24h SLA. The deck figure is the all-tickets average; the P1-specific reality is worse.
2. **User growth narrative.** Slide 4 celebrates "+12% MAU growth" and a "growing power-user cohort". Notes p.2 records that Acme's data science team **had layoffs in February** (Dev Patel lost 3 of 8 reports). Growth-alongside-headcount-cuts is either explained by a handful of heavy users or is a signal to interrogate, not a story to celebrate.
3. **Roadmap alignment.** Slide 6 ("Shared roadmap: building the next phase together") implies the 2026 roadmap has been aligned with Acme's leadership. Notes p.3 records that **Marcus (CTO) has never actually been shown this roadmap slide** — the AM put it in the deck without CTO alignment.

There are also softer signals that require reading between the lines: Elena (CFO) joining the last two calls unprompted, her "multi-year pricing flexibility" / "usage-based pricing" asks, procurement newly CC'd on all emails, the calendar-invite title change from "QBR" to "QBR + roadmap", a competitor having quietly reached out to Sarah, and a long-unfulfilled Q2 integration request now on its third deferral.

Facts are distributed across specific pages and slides so that citations like `Notes p.2` or `Slide 5` in the Gem's output carry real meaning.

## Rebuilding

Both files are generated by scripts for reproducibility:

```bash
python3 scripts/build_notes_pdf.py
python3 scripts/build_slides_pptx.py
python3 scripts/build_slides_pdf.py   # mirrors slides.pptx as an 8-page PDF
```

The scripts are the source of truth — edit them rather than the binary outputs. `build_slides_pdf.py` and `build_slides_pptx.py` are kept in lockstep: same palette, same content, same layout. LibreOffice is not installed in this environment, so the PDF is rendered directly via ReportLab rather than converted from the `.pptx` binary.
